SUPPLY CHAIN

From Collection to Impact

We bridge the gap between donors and beneficiaries with a streamlined and traceable process — ensuring every contribution makes a measurable difference.

Collection

Materials such as used notebooks, pens, and partially filled stationery are gathered through eco-drives organized in schools, housing societies, and offices. These campaigns not only collect valuable items but also spread awareness about sustainable disposal. Each item is logged, ensuring transparency from the first step.

Sorting

Dedicated volunteers manually inspect and classify each item into categories: reusable, recyclable, or decomposable. This sorting process ensures only safe and quality materials move forward in the supply chain while reducing contamination or waste. Special care is taken to separate fragile or damaged goods.

Transportation

Items are carefully packed and dispatched via eco-friendly transport such as e-rickshaws, cycles, and vans. These materials are routed to processing hubs or community warehouses depending on their type. Routes are optimized to reduce emissions and delivery time.

Processing

Each item undergoes a thorough quality check. Usable notebooks are rebound, pens are cleaned and refilled, and sorted paper is bundled. Faulty materials are responsibly recycled. This adds immense value by giving waste a second life.

Reuse

Processed items are assembled into educational kits including notebooks, stationery, and art materials. These are distributed to underprivileged students via NGOs and schools, empowering learning and reducing the need for new manufacturing.

Impact Tracking

Each donation is traceable using a unique tracking ID. Contributors can view the final destination of their items, building trust and encouraging repeat participation. The journey of every product is transparent and meaningful.
